
exploration:
  - Research question is focused and relevant
  - Background information is cited and scientifically accurate
  - Methodology directly addresses the research question
  - Variables and controls are clearly defined and justified

analysis:
  - Raw data is sufficient and presented clearly
  - Appropriate statistics are used (mean, SD, SEM, t-test)
  - Measurement uncertainties are acknowledged and incorporated
  - Data is interpreted in context of the hypothesis

evaluation:
  - Conclusion is supported by the data and answers the research question
  - Compared to accepted scientific context
  - Weaknesses and methodological limitations are analyzed
  - Improvements and extensions are suggested

communication:
  - Report is well-structured and easy to follow
  - Appropriate scientific terminology is used correctly
  - Tables and graphs include units and uncertainties
  - Citations are correctly formatted
